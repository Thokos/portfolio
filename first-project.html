Why this project?

Every time when my (hockey) team has a training or a game to play we spam Whatsapp with a long list of who can attend the training/game and who can’t. But even though we spam Whatsapp with this list there are still some questions remaining:

  • Are we playing in our home or away jersey?

  • At what time do we play?

  • At what time do we gather?

This gets annoying pretty fast and these questions offer new possibilities and another solution to this problem. Also what’s app isn’t the best place to display/discuss these things. That’s why I came up with the idea to make an application that is gonna solve all these questions.

How I started

I started by writing down the screens I thought that needed to be done. Afterward, I wrote down the functionalities and my goals for this application.

After the overview was done, and I got a good idea of how the screens needed to be designed I started to separate the screens into sprints. This will make the work easier to digest. Subsequently, this made it possible to set a deadline.

Every sprint contains the screens I planned on doing. First I made sketches of the screens, afterward, it was time to make some low fidelity screens which later on translated into the high fidelity screens.

1. First sprint

For the first sprint the login screen, creating an account, and creating a team was on the menu. First I summarized the content needed for every screen (for example inputs, headers, buttons needed). When I was done doing that I started sketching these screens and made the ideas I had about this app come to reality but not with too much detail yet.

After the sketches, I made a user flow to display how the user is gonna move through the first few screens.

Then it was time to turn the sketches into low fidelity screens. I used Figma for this task(all the low and high fidelity screens are done in Figma).

Before I started with the high fidelity screens I had to know what font I was gonna use, which colors, and the other input styles. That's why I decided to start with a small branding book to set the rules for The Hockeyplanner App.

3. Third Sprint

Sprint 3 is where everything started to come together. First I started evaluating what needed to be done for every screen. I'll start with the leaderboard screen since that one will need some explanation.

The leaderboard screen was the most challenging because I wanted to use some kind of gamification on this app to motivate people to set their availability. That's the main purpose of the app that people will let other teammates know if they are attending the training/game or not. In games nowadays seasons and battle passes (battle passes contain some rewards you get after playing a certain game.) are very common. They give rewards to people for completing tasks to motivate them to get certain behavior done.

Above you can see how you can collect these points. I did some research on what kind of activity (training/match) the availability was the lowest. People attended games way more (approx. 35% more) than they did to a training. So I decided as a solution to give out more points for attending a training than a match. You can also score goals for assists given and goals scored. Which the captain can fill in right after the match has been played.

Since most competitive sports already work with a season it's a good thing to add this feature in the Hockey Planner App as well. So the team captain can set a reward and a timer for the start of the season and the end of the season. This cannot be changed once set so there won't be any cheating.

On top of that, I decided that people needed more motivation so designed some badges which all the players can earn if they complete certain tasks (for example; score 100 points). Below you see the sketches, wireframes and high fidelity screens I made for this solution.

Settings were also on the menu for sprint 3 but this screen is pretty straightforward with the most important function visible for every player.

My final words


What I learned

I learned quite a few things from this project. One of the things was making a switch from Sketch to Figma. Figma is a hot and upcoming program and I like the UI, this is the first full project I did in Figma.

I learned how to brainstorm and make pages from the text on the paper to a working prototype.

I also read a lot about gamification lately and this was a good project to try this new knowledge I just had learned.

What would I do different next time

I will most certainly work with components on my next project. At first, I thought I could do without it but when I was in the middle of the project I decided to change the inputs and buttons. I could have saved a lot of time if I just made some components at the start.

Also when I'm making a prototype I won't do it till the very end. I found a lot of edge cases when I was prototyping.
