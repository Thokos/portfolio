Why this project?

Everytime when my (hockey) team has a training or a game to play we spam whatsapp with a long list with who can attend the training/game and who can’t. But even though we spam what's app with this list there are still some questions remaining:

  • Are we playing in our home or away jersey?

  • At what time do we play?

  • At what time do we gather?

This gets annoying pretty fast and these questions offer new possibilities and another solution to this problem. Also what’s app isn’t the best place to display/discuss these things. That’s why I came up with the idea to make an application which is gonna solve all these questions.

How I started

I started by writing down the screens I thought that needed to be done. Afterwards I wrote down the functionality of the application and what want to achieve with the app.

After the overview was done and I got a good idea of what screens needed to be designed I started to divide the screens into sprints to make the work easier to digest. This also made it possible to set a deadline for every sprint so I finished the things I worked on.

Every sprint contains the screens I planned on doing. Firstly I made sketches of the screens, afterwards it was time to make some low fidelity screens which later on translated in the high fidelty screens.

1. First sprint

For the first sprint the login screen, creating an account and creating a team was on the menu. First I summarized the content needed for every screen (for example inputs, headers, buttons needed). When I was done doing that I started sketching these screens and made the ideas I had about this app come to reality but not with too much detail yet.

After the sketches I made a user flow to display how the user is gonna move through the first few screens.

Then it was time to turn the sketches into low fidelty screens. I used figma for this task(all the low and high fidelity screens are done in Figma).

Before I started with the high fidelty screens I had to know what font I was gonna use, which colors and the other input styles. That's why I decided to start with a small branding book to set the rules for The Hockeyplanner App.
